# Beginner learning validation protocol

## Claim under test

After completing System Forge chapter 1, a learner with no prior LLD practice can transfer the ownership idea to a different domain and explain why the decision contains change.

The product does not claim this is true yet. The in-app study collects the evidence needed to evaluate it.

## Participant criteria

- At least five participants who report no prior LLD practice.
- Use a fresh browser profile or cleared local progress.
- Do not coach, define responsibility, or explain the correct class owner before the baseline answer.
- Pilot sessions from experienced users or devices with prior System Forge evidence stay in the export but are excluded from first-time outcome calculations.

## Protocol

1. Open Progress, then Beginner learning check.
2. Record baseline confidence and answer the Library Shelf transfer prompt.
3. Start System Forge chapter 1 without additional explanation.
4. Complete the run, failure, repair, transfer, and free-form defense.
5. Return to the study page and answer the same transfer problem after the lesson.
6. Record confidence and an optional confusion note.
7. Export the anonymous JSON and combine it with other participant exports outside the product.

## Instrumented measures

- first-time eligibility;
- verified mission completion after baseline;
- time from study start to opening the lesson;
- total completion time;
- baseline and post-transfer rubric scores;
- confidence change;
- free-form confusion note.

The rubric checks whether the answer names Shelf as owner, connects search behavior to Shelf-owned books, keeps Catalog coordinating across shelves, contains future storage changes, and gives a causal reason. It is a product-learning rubric, not an interview or hiring score.

## Decision thresholds for the first round

Do not describe the beginner experience as validated until all conditions hold across at least five eligible sessions:

- 80% or more open the canonical mission within 60 seconds;
- 80% or more complete without facilitator intervention;
- median post-transfer score improves by at least 20 points;
- 80% or more reach at least 80/100 on the post-transfer rubric;
- no repeated confusion theme appears in more than one participant without a documented product response.

If a threshold fails, treat it as a design finding, change the smallest responsible surface, and rerun with new first-time participants. Never merge pilot and eligible sessions to make a result look stronger.
