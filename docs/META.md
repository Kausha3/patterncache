# Meta Software Engineering interview pack

Last researched: 2026-07-18

## Evidence policy

This pack does not turn public anecdotes into fake frequency statistics. It uses three evidence levels:

1. **Official scope or example**: Meta's own full-loop guide names the interview area, evaluation signal, or example prompt.
2. **Selected recent report**: a first-person account can show that a format exists, but it cannot establish frequency.
3. **Transfer drill**: a lesson covers an official evaluation dimension without claiming Meta asks that exact prompt.

The product's four-dot signal is qualitative source confidence, not a probability that a prompt will appear.

## Primary source

- [Meta Careers: Preparing for Your Full Loop Interview](https://www.metacareers.com/careers/SWE-prep-onsite) links the official guide and says the loop consists of several conversations designed to assess technical skills and mutual fit.
- [Meta Software Engineer Full Loop Interview Guide (official PDF)](https://d3no4ktch0fdq4.cloudfront.net/public/course/files/Meta_SWE_full_loop_guide.pdf) is the source of truth for this pack.

The guide states:

- the full loop contains 4 to 6 conversations of about 45 minutes across coding, design, and behavioral interviews;
- coding is assessed on communication, problem solving, coding, and verification;
- candidates should expect about two coding problems in about 40 minutes, practice without a compiler, and prioritize breadth;
- the guide explicitly says not to spend preparation time on dynamic programming for this loop;
- design may be Systems Design or Product Architectural Design, so candidates should confirm the exact round with their recruiter;
- design is assessed on problem navigation, solution design, technical excellence, and technical communication;
- official system-design examples include a URL shortener, Facebook Chat, and worldwide video distribution;
- official product-architecture examples include a product or service API, chat or feed API, and email server;
- behavioral assessment names five signals: resolving conflict, growing continuously, embracing ambiguity, driving results, and communicating effectively.

## How the app translates the source

- URL Shortener, Chat, Feed API, video distribution, and product API are labeled official examples or official scope.
- Cache is labeled a transfer drill because it exercises the guide's scalability, availability, performance, and operational criteria but is not named as an example prompt.
- No separate LLD list is promoted. Meta's official guide describes Systems Design and Product Architectural Design, not a default object-oriented LLD round.
- The mock interviewer uses the five official behavioral signals plus a technical-excellence dimension derived from the guide's coding and design criteria.
- The pack uses Meta's E3-E6 naming instead of Amazon or Google's L-level labels.

## Important caveats

- Recruiter packets and role-specific loops can differ. Confirm the number and type of interviews, preferred coding language, and whether design is systems or product architecture with the recruiter.
- A public guide establishes expected evaluation behavior, not exact question frequency.
- Recent process experiments, including AI-assisted coding, may be role- or cohort-specific. They are deliberately not encoded as a universal requirement without official packet confirmation.
